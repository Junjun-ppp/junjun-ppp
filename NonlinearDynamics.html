Nonlinear Dynamics

Here we will learn about nonlinear dynamics and chaos. The following literature has been prepared for reference.

  1. S. H. Strogatz, Nonlinear dynamics and chaos: with applications to physics, biology, chemistry, and engineering. (CRC Press, ed. 2nd, 2019), pp. xiii, 513 p.
  2. I. Chiba, Differential equations and dynamical system theory for solving differential equations. (Gendai Math., 2021), pp. 253 p.
  3. A. Kaneko, Lectures on Differential Equations. Library Lectures on Mathematics for Mathematical and Informational Sciences (Science, 2014), vol. 4, pp. iv, 257p.
  4. K. Nakamura, Chaos and quantum physics : Challenging the intersection of paradigms. Bessatsu Mathematical Sciences (Science, 1997), vol. . SGC Library ; 1, pp. iv, 137p.

Basically, we follow [1]. We have tried to make it possible to read the book in a narrative manner by discarding topics, exercises, etc. from [1]. I also referred to [2] and [3] for mathematical descriptions that I thought were necessary in some places. I would like to go into the content of [4], but it does not look like I will be able to get there.

The purpose of this material is only to refine the way of dealing with nonlinear dynamics and intuition, not to develop a mathematically rigorous theory. We plan to prepare a document on dynamical systems theory that develops a more mathematically rigorous approach. The ultimate goal is to put the structure together as a Hamiltonian systems theory.

Chapter 1 Introduction to Differential Equations

In this section, after considering what differential equations are, explanations of terms and so on are given that will be necessary for future discussions.

Chapter 2 Quadrature

In Chapter 2, so-called "solvable" differential equations are treated, and different methods of solving them are studied by means of examples.

Chapter 3 Existence Theorems for Solutions

In Chapter 3, we consider the existence of solutions to differential equations, in particular the existence of unique solutions. This chapter is theoretically important in order to consider more abstract differential equations in the future.

Chapter 4: Linear systems of differential equations　(Working on it!)

In Chapter 4, we discuss linear differential equations at a higher level of abstraction.
